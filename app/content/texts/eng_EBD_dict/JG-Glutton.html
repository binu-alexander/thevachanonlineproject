GLUTTON

"GLUTTON "(Deuteronomy 21:20), Hebrews zolel, from a word meaning“to shake out,” “to squander;” and hence one who is prodigal, who wastes his means by indulgence. In Proverbs 23:21, the word means debauchees or wasters of their own body. In Proverbs 28:7, the word (pl.) is rendered Authorized Version “riotous men;” Revised Version, “gluttonous.” Matthew 11:19, Luke 7:34, Greek phagos, given to eating, gluttonous.
